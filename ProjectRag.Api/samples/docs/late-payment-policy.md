# Late Payment Policy

Invoices are due 30 calendar days after the invoice date unless a contract states a different payment term.

A payment is considered late when funds have not been received by the end of the due date. Late balances may receive a 1.5 percent monthly fee after a 10 day grace period.

Customers with two or more late payments in a rolling six month period may be moved to prepaid billing. Account managers should notify the customer before changing billing terms.

Late fees may be waived when the delay was caused by a billing error, duplicate invoice, incorrect purchase order, or documented payment processing outage.

Collections escalation begins 45 days after the due date. Legal escalation requires finance director approval.
